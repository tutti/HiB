Pigeonhole principle:
Let f: f(A->B) (A, B finite sets)
If |A| > |B|, then at least one value of f occurs more than once.
    There does not exist an injective function on finite sets where the domain is larger than the codomain.
More general:
If |A| > k*|B| k in N, then there is a value of f that occurs at least k+1 times.
